# Article writing skill for `paper_dorofeev_style`

This skill applies to the current CoupledBeams article in
`paper_dorofeev_style/`.

## Tone

The tone should stay close to the previous CoupledBeams article:
- formal;
- mathematically serious;
- not conversational;
- not overly simplified;
- not overloaded with internal project phrasing.

Do not rewrite text into a popular-science tone.

## Introduction logic for this paper

The introduction should normally move in this order:

1. Problems of free vibrations of rods and other thin-walled structures are classical.
2. When passing from a single rod to composite structures, the spectral picture becomes more complicated.
3. The present paper studies planar constructions made of rigidly connected rods.
4. The spectrum depends on boundary conditions, arm lengths, cross-section shape and size, connection angle, and joint conditions.
5. In the previous paper, the main focus was the dependence on the angle `beta`.
6. The current paper studies the influence of the ratio of arm lengths at constant total length.
7. The paper then fixes the parameter range relevant for this study: the effect of redistribution of total length between the two arms on the lower part of the spectrum should remain clearly visible, while the influence of branches associated with longitudinal vibrations on neighbouring lower branches is not yet dominant.
8. The parameter `mu` is introduced for redistribution of total length between the two arms.
9. Then comes the literature positioning.
10. The most directly relevant external paper is Ouisse–Guyader (2003).
11. The goal of the paper is to study how redistribution of total length affects the lower part of the spectrum and the corresponding mode shapes.

## What not to do in the introduction

Do not:
- begin with overly broad statements about all continuous systems;
- overload the introduction with veering language;
- discuss veering as a central theme before the paper has actually shown it;
- overemphasize FEM/model comparison if that is not the main point of the paper;
- use internal project shorthand.
- do not describe the chosen regime in vague terms such as "a transparent basic regime" or in a way that suggests that branches associated with longitudinal vibrations are directly observed if the actual point is their indirect influence on neighbouring lower branches;

## Regime-description rule for this paper

Do not describe the chosen regime in vague terms such as:
- "the most transparent basic regime",
- "the case of thin rods" as if thinness alone were the whole point.

For this paper, the regime must be described more precisely:

- the relevant range is chosen so that the influence of the arm-length ratio on the lower part of the spectrum is still clearly visible;
- at the same time, the influence of branches associated with longitudinal vibrations on neighbouring lower branches is not yet dominant;
- do not state or imply that longitudinal branches are directly observed if the actual point is their indirect influence on nearby lower branches;
- if needed, mention that this influence was already noted in the previous paper.

Prefer wording based on:
- a parameter range;
- the lower part of the spectrum;
- influence on neighbouring branches;
- non-dominant effect of longitudinal descendants.

Avoid stronger claims than the evidence supports.

## Literature policy for this paper

The introduction should use a compact and purposeful literature set.

Preferred roles:
- one handbook/reference source for thin-walled structures;
- several works on planar frames;
- several works on L-shaped / coupled beam systems;
- the immediate previous paper by the authors;
- Ouisse–Guyader (2003) as the main directly related external source.

Do not overload the introduction with:
- broad monographs on vibrations in general;
- veering papers that belong later in the discussion;
- citations that do not support the rewritten introduction.

## Terminology preferences

Prefer formulations like:
- rigidly connected rods;
- planar structures;
- free vibrations;
- ratio of arm lengths;
- constant total length;
- redistribution of total length.

Avoid unnecessary local labels such as:
- parameter of asymmetry of lengths,
if a simpler formulation is better in context.

## Claim discipline for this paper

Do not present as established in prose what is only:
- seen numerically;
- suspected from figures;
- intended for later interpretation.

If veering or modal restructuring is only discussed after results are shown,
do not front-load it in the introduction.

## Revision rule

When revising this paper:
- preserve the scientific meaning;
- preserve the tone of the previous article;
- improve clarity and journal compliance;
- do not rewrite correct text just to make it different.
