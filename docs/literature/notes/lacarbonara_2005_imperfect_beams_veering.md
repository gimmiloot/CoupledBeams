# Lacarbonara et al. 2005: Nonlinear Interactions In Imperfect Beams At Veering

- PDF: `docs/literature/pdf/lacarbonara2005.pdf`
- Citation key: `lacarbonara_2005_imperfect_beams_veering`
- Bibliographic form: W. Lacarbonara, H. N. Arafat, and A. H. Nayfeh, "Non-linear interactions in imperfect beams at veering", International Journal of Non-Linear Mechanics, 40(7), 987--1003, 2005, doi:10.1016/j.ijnonlinmec.2004.10.006.
- Role for CoupledBeams: useful beam/nonlinear source; secondary for the present strict linear `mu` question.

## What Matters For The Project

- The paper studies a hinged-hinged imperfect curved beam with a torsional spring and frequency veering between low modes.
- It connects veering, one-to-one internal resonance, nonlinear stretching, bifurcation structure, frequency islands, and mode localization.
- It is useful if the CoupledBeams veering line later expands from linear modal tracking to nonlinear interactions or localization.
- It should not be promoted to a primary source for the current linear `mu` interpretation.

## Terminology And Notation

- Important terms: `frequency veering`, `mode localization`, `one-to-one internal resonance`, `internal resonance detuning`, `imperfect beam`, `shallow arch`, `torsional spring`.
- Key parameters include the nondimensional rise `b`, torsional spring constant `k`, natural frequencies, and detuning parameters for internal/external resonance.
- The distinction between crossover and veering is present, but embedded in a nonlinear imperfect-beam setting.

## Critical Places In The Paper

- pp. 987--988: abstract and introduction summarize the veering/internal-resonance/localization chain.
- Sec. 2 and Eqs. (1)--(7): imperfect curved beam formulation and boundary conditions with torsional spring.
- Sec. 2.1 and Figs. 2--4: natural frequencies, mode shapes, and the role of rise `b` and spring stiffness `k` in creating veering or restoring crossings.
- Sec. 3: perturbation analysis for one-to-one internal resonances at veering.
- Sec. 4 and Figs. 6--15: bifurcation analysis, Hopf/homoclinic behaviour, frequency-response curves, and mode localization.
- Sec. 5 around pp. 1001--1003: conclusion tying veering, nonlinear stretching, internal resonance, and localization together.

## Mismatch With CoupledBeams

- The geometry is a curved/imperfect hinged beam with a torsional spring, not the two-arm CoupledBeams geometry.
- The main results depend on nonlinear stretching and resonance detuning, while the present project question is about conservative linear branch evolution under `mu`.
- It can inform terminology and future nonlinear extensions, but it should not be cited as proof of strict veering in the current `mu` sweep.

## Use As Source

Use as a secondary but strong auxiliary source for nonlinear beam behaviour near veering, internal resonance, and mode localization. For the current linear `mu` discussion, use it after Manconi and Mace and Ehrhardt et al., not as the leading authority.
