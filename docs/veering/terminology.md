# Terminology

## Veering

Working meaning: two nearby eigenfrequency branches approach as a parameter
changes, avoid a true crossing, and separate again while their mode shapes
change rapidly in the interaction region.

For the current project, a strict veering claim should be based on tracked
branch identity, a close spectral gap, and mode-shape evidence.

## Quasi-Degeneracy

Working meaning: a cautious label for close or nearly close spectral situations
with rapid modal reorganization where strict veering has not been established.

For this project, use this term when the data show rapid changes of local
modal order, nodal pattern, or localization, but do not yet provide a convincing
competing tracked branch, small paired gap, and pairwise exchange evidence.

The plate-specific symmetry-group rules from `nair_1973_quasi_degeneracies`
should not be transferred directly to the coupled-rod geometry.

## Exchange Of Modal Character

Working meaning: an identified branch keeps its continuation identity, but the
spatial or physical character of its mode changes in a way that resembles an
exchange with a nearby branch or local modal family.

For this project, the main observable is the change of local modal order on
the left and right arms, not just the sorted frequency index.

## Mode Localization

Working meaning: the mode shape becomes concentrated mainly on one part of the
system or one arm, rather than being comparably distributed over both arms.

For this project, localization should be supported by shape or energy measures
on the two arms, not only by visual inspection.

## Missing Evidence For Strict Claims

- Quantified minimum spectral gaps between candidate branches.
- A tracked comparison branch for each suspected exchange.
- Modal assurance / correlation data across the suspected interaction window.
- Arm-wise energy or amplitude localization metrics.
- A check that the observed change is not only a relabeling by sorted
  frequency order.
