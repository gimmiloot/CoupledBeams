# Literature Assessment

This note separates similarity by mechanism from similarity by geometry.

## `manconi_2017_veering_strong_coupling`

- Mechanism: central source for distinguishing rapid veering under weak
  coupling from slow eigenvalue-locus evolution under strong coupling.
- Geometry: general structural-dynamics treatment with discrete and continuous
  examples, not a direct two-rod geometry match.
- Current use: primary theoretical source for cautious language around the
  current `mu` sweep: when a close tracked pair and rapid exchange are absent,
  wording should move toward slow evolution / modal-character reorganization,
  not strict veering.

## `ehrhardt_2018_clamped_beam_veering`

- Mechanism: strong beam-like example where symmetry-preserving tuning gives
  crossing and symmetry-breaking tuning gives veering with mode-shape mixing.
- Geometry: a clamped beam with a perpendicular cross-beam and movable masses;
  closer to beam mechanics than plate examples, but still not the present
  coupled-rod determinant.
- Current use: best close beam analogue for crossing versus veering,
  eigenvector correlation / self-MAC, and the need for mode-shape evidence.

## `lacarbonara_2005_imperfect_beams_veering`

- Mechanism: useful for veering, internal resonance, nonlinear interactions,
  bifurcation structure, and localization in an imperfect beam.
- Geometry: beam source, but curved/imperfect and torsionally restrained rather
  than two rigidly connected rods.
- Current use: secondary source for nonlinear/localization context. Do not use
  it as the main source for the current strict linear `mu` question.

## `fontanela_2021_nonlinear_localisation_coupled_beams`

- Mechanism: useful source for nonlinear localization in a symmetric two-beam
  system and for interpreting arm-wise localization.
- Geometry: two weakly coupled cantilever beams are geometrically suggestive,
  but the decisive mechanism is clearance/contact nonlinearity.
- Current use: localization source only; not primary evidence for strict linear
  veering in the `mu` sweep.

## `perkins1986`

- Mechanism: relevant as a source on veering in eigenvalue problems and on
  rapid changes of eigenvectors near close spectral branches.
- Geometry: not a direct analogue of the present two-arm coupled-beam geometry.
- Current use: background for interpreting branch proximity and modal-character
  changes, not evidence that the present system already has veering.

## `pierre1988`

- Mechanism: relevant for mode localization and its relation to veering in
  weakly coupled or nearly periodic systems.
- Geometry: not a direct analogue of the present rigidly connected two-rod
  geometry.
- Current use: background for the localization / modal-exchange vocabulary.

## `liu2002`

- Mechanism: relevant as broader spectral-sensitivity background, especially
  where frequency derivatives or nearby modes are discussed.
- Geometry: not established as a direct analogue of the current system.
- Current use: supporting literature only; do not cite it as a geometry match
  unless a later source check justifies that.

## `nair_1973_quasi_degeneracies`

- Mechanism: useful as a source for the vocabulary of frequency crossings,
  transitions / quasi-degeneracies, and rapid modal or nodal-pattern changes
  near close branches.
- Geometry: plate vibration problems, not a direct analogue of the present
  rigidly connected two-rod geometry.
- Current use: supporting literature for cautious wording. It strengthens the
  interpretation that rapid modal change without a close competing tracked pair
  and pairwise exchange evidence is better described as quasi-degeneracy-like
  or modal-character reorganization, not strict veering.

## Working Conclusion

The literature now gives a stronger mechanism-level basis than a geometry-level
match. Manconi and Mace should anchor the distinction between strict rapid
veering and slow evolution under stronger coupling. Ehrhardt et al. give the
best beam-like analogue for symmetry breaking, crossing versus veering, and
mode-shape mixing. Lacarbonara et al. and Fontanela et al. are useful mainly for
nonlinear interaction and localization context.

For the current system, these sources justify careful checking of branch
identity, close paired gaps, mode-shape correlation, localization, and
quasi-degeneracy-like reorganization. They do not by themselves prove strict
veering or modal exchange in any selected CoupledBeams branch.
