# Literature Assessment

This note separates similarity by mechanism from similarity by geometry.

## `perkins1986`

- Mechanism: relevant as a source on veering in eigenvalue problems and on
  rapid changes of eigenvectors near close spectral branches.
- Geometry: not a direct analogue of the present two-arm coupled-beam geometry.
- Current use: background for interpreting branch proximity and modal-character
  changes, not evidence that the present system already has veering.

## `pierre1988`

- Mechanism: relevant for mode localization and its relation to veering in
  weakly coupled or nearly periodic systems.
- Geometry: not a direct analogue of the present rigidly connected two-rod
  geometry.
- Current use: background for the localization / modal-exchange vocabulary.

## `liu2002`

- Mechanism: relevant as broader spectral-sensitivity background, especially
  where frequency derivatives or nearby modes are discussed.
- Geometry: not established as a direct analogue of the current system.
- Current use: supporting literature only; do not cite it as a geometry match
  unless a later source check justifies that.

## Working Conclusion

The three sources are useful mainly by mechanism, not by geometry. For the
current system, they justify careful checking of branch identity, mode shapes,
and localization, but they do not by themselves prove veering or modal exchange
in any selected branch.
